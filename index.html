Saumya Jetley

TAU Team, INRIA-Paris Saclay       saumya.jetley@inria.fr

I am a postdoc with Michele Sebag at the TAU Team, INRIA-Paris Saclay. I work on causal inference in the context of French job market, alongside the industrial partner QAPA, and on privacy-preserving predictive analysis for French covid data. I completed my PhD in computer vision and machine learning algorithms for AI systems, with a special focus on interpretability and robustness, under Phil Torr at Oxford University.

My research work has been variously funded by the Government of India, European research council, US department of defense and Five AI (autonomous driving startup). I am an IET Postgraduate Research award holder for 2019, have published several papers in top-tier CV and ML conferences, was selected for the FDL NASA programme 2019, and was an invited panel member at FDL Europe Big Think 2020. I have presented my research at various international conferences (NeurIPS, CVPR, ICLR) and workshops (Women in Machine Learning, Deep Learning Edinburgh, Reconfigured Vision), and am grateful to have been an invited speaker at institutes such as AI Wadhwani, ISI Kolkata, IIT Kharagpur, INRIA Paris Central, INRIA Paris Saclay.

Datasets

Long long time ago, I gathered a dataset of 'Country Flags in the Wild'. It contains 12,854 train images and 6,110 test images of the flags of 224 different countries harvested from the world wide web and manually cropped to loosely fit to the inlying flags. More details can be found in this paper. To get access to the dataset, please get in touch with me by email. The demo of our android app using an ML model trained on this dataset can found here.
